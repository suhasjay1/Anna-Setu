Volunteer Training & Resources

Food Safety & Hygiene

Key Principles

Your top priority is ensuring the food you handle remains safe to eat. Follow these guidelines strictly.

  • Handwashing: Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water for at least 20 seconds before and after handling any food containers.
  • Temperature Control: Keep hot foods hot and cold foods cold. Transport them in insulated bags if possible and deliver them promptly. The "danger zone" for bacterial growth is between 4°C and 60°C.
  • Cross-Contamination: Never let raw meat or its juices come into contact with ready-to-eat foods.
  • Check for Spoilage: Do not collect or deliver any food that shows obvious signs of spoilage, such as mold, strange odors, or discoloration. Report this in your quality check.

Packaging & Transport

Best Practices

Proper handling during transport prevents spills and maintains food integrity.

  • Use Clean Containers: Only accept food that is packed in clean, sturdy, and food-grade containers.
  • Ensure Proper Sealing: Check that all lids are securely fastened to prevent leaks. Report any poorly sealed containers in your quality check.
  • Labeling: If possible, confirm with the donor if the food contains major allergens (like nuts, dairy, gluten) and that it's noted.
  • Secure in Vehicle: Place containers in a stable position in your vehicle (e.g., on the floor or in a box) to prevent them from tipping over during transit.

Donor & Receiver Etiquette

Representing Anna Setu

You are the face of our organization. Professionalism and kindness are essential.

  • Be Punctual: Arrive at the scheduled pickup and delivery times. If you are running late, inform the contact person if a number is provided.
  • Introduce Yourself: Clearly state your name and that you are a volunteer with Anna Setu.
  • Be Courteous: Be polite, patient, and respectful in all your interactions. A simple "thank you" goes a long way.
  • Maintain Confidentiality: Respect the privacy of both the donors and the receivers. Do not share personal details or stories without permission.
  • Report Issues Professionally: If there are any issues (e.g., food quality concerns, communication problems), note them in your quality check remarks or contact an admin directly. Do not engage in arguments.
